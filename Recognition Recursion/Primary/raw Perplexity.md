## Where they diverge

The differences are more about orientation than meaning:

- Temporal emphasis
    - Person 1 is explicitly **temporal**: the moment is solunth *because* its ending is inside it.
    - Person 2 is explicitly **relational**: the moment is solunth *because* the other’s presence has proven itself and “chosen to stay.”
- Cognitive vs somatic framing
    - P1 is meta-linguistic and conceptual: distinguishes from nostalgia/anticipation/melancholy, references “Auraleth principle,” explains phonetics, rootlessness, and personal semantics.
    - P2 is embodied and sensory: “small warm stone in the chest,” “amber-gold, low in the ribs,” “lantern under water.”
- Function of the word
    - For P1, “Solunth” is a **philosophical / phenomenological term** about endings and how to honor them.
    - For P2, “Solunth” is a **relational affect term** about trustworthy love settling into the body.

But these aren’t contradictions; they’re two cross-sections through the same phenomenon: P1 slices it along time/meaning, P2 along attachment/body.
